## Peer Blog Audit Criteria

### Content Assessment

1. Does the post accurately represent course concepts and readings?
2. Is the argument clear, coherent, and well-structured?
3. Does the post demonstrate critical thinking and analysis (not just summary)?
4. Are the required questions from both the course outline and instructor posts addressed?
5. Does the post include relevant Canadian context where appropriate?

### Quality Assessment

6. Does the post include a clear, descriptive title?
7. Is a featured image included for visual engagement?
8. Are hyperlinks included to relevant concepts, resources, and people?
9. Is media (images, videos, audio) properly integrated with alt text?
10. Are all content sources appropriately attributed?
11. Is the post organized with descriptive headings (H2, H3)?
12. Is the post assigned to at least one category?

### Feedback to Provide

13. What does the post do well? (specific examples)
14. Where does the author show strong understanding?
15. What additional resources could strengthen the post?
16. What connections to other course topics could be made?
17. Are there areas where the analysis could go deeper?

### Tone Guidelines

- Be specific and actionable
- Frame suggestions positively
- Reference course materials where relevant
- Be encouraging while being honest
