## Book Overview

*One Without the Other* presents a vision of inclusive education through storytelling. Moore and Schnellert draw on their experiences as educators in British Columbia to illustrate how inclusive practices benefit all learners, not just those with identified needs. The book challenges the traditional approach of separating students with disabilities from their peers and advocates for a model where diversity is seen as a strength rather than a challenge.

## Key Themes (Introduction & Part 1)

- The case for inclusive education as beneficial for all students
- Stories of how diverse classrooms strengthen learning for everyone
- Challenging the "one without the other" binary -- inclusion is not about choosing between supporting individual needs and serving the whole class
- The role of community in creating inclusive learning environments
- How diversity enhances rather than hinders educational outcomes
- Practical strategies for inclusive practice in Canadian schools
