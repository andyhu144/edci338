## Blog Post #5: PLN & Education

**Due:** Sunday, February 22, 2026

This week examines how PLNs function in professional and educational contexts. We explore how educators use social media for professional development, the role of social media in education, and the challenges that arise when working with vulnerable populations.

### Required Materials

- WATCH: [Education in a Connected Culture](./education-connected-culture.md)
- READ: [Developing Personal Learning Networks for Open and Social Learning - Couros, Ch. 6](./couros-developing-plns.md)

### Questions to Address (merged from both sources)

**From Course Outline:**
- Community communications go beyond blogs and social media shares -- how does a PLN help and hinder development of thoughts and ideas in education discourse?
- How do educators create discourse?
- What is the role of social media in education?
- What are some problems with social media communications in education settings?
- Which social media platforms are beneficial in education?
- When working with the vulnerable sector, how does social media fit into professionalism and regulations?

**From Instructor Posts Page:**
- Explore themes from the "Education in a Connected Culture" video
- Identify beneficial platforms in education
- How does social media fit into professionalism when working with vulnerable populations?

### Key Concepts

- **Open teaching:** Making course content, activities, and participation open to the public (Couros)
- **Connectivism:** Learning theory emphasizing networks and connections (Siemens)
- **PLN as outcome:** Personal learning networks as both a process and result of open, networked learning
- **Professional boundaries:** Navigating social media use when working with vulnerable populations
