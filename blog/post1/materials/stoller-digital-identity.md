Well digital identity is one of those things that can be a bit nebulous. I would define it as your presence online -- it's everything that you post, share, how you are, how you engage, how you treat other people, and how you use digital tools for your own benefit as well as for the benefit of the people that you work with or teach or educate, that type of thing.

Your digital identity -- it's sort of your footprint on the web. It's this thing that is with you, that is searchable -- your body of work, your portfolio, your blog, your tweets. Everything that you do on the web kind of represents your digital identity. And so if you're posting things that a potential employer might see and say "this is somebody that we really want to hire because look at the way they're writing, look at the way they're creating, they're designing, they're showcasing who they are" -- which is almost a pre-interview in a sense.

And on the flip side of that, of course, is there's people who've had their careers dented or diminished because they posted things that have been detrimental to their success, because they've said things that they shouldn't have or said things that weren't -- they maybe were lacking context.
