## Questions to Address in Blog Post #1

### From Course Outline

1. What does it mean to network using social media?
2. How are we motivated to participate in networked publics?
3. What are the risks & rewards of public communications?
4. What is a digital identity?
5. How do personal versus professional approaches to digital identity affect social media use?
6. How do digital identities converge in networked publics -- what are the impacts and/or benefits?

### From Instructor Posts Page

7. What constitutes digital identity?
8. How do personal versus professional digital identities differ in social media contexts?
9. What risks and rewards accompany public communications?

### Digital Identity Exercise

10. Reflect on your controlled and uncontrolled social media presence -- privately source and identify your social media content that you have either created or have had shared. Discuss with your learning pod.
    - **Controlled presence:** What have you intentionally posted/created?
    - **Uncontrolled presence:** What exists about you online that you did not create or control?

### Suggested Reflection Points

- Consider Eric Stoller's definition of digital identity as your "footprint on the web"
- Reflect on danah boyd's concept of networked privacy -- how does your data affect others?
- Think about the economic value of digital identity as discussed in the WEF panel
- How does your digital identity serve you professionally? Could it hinder you?
