## Blog Post #7: PLN & the Evolving Internet

**Due:** Sunday, March 8, 2026

This week focuses on how PLNs intersect with AI, equity, accessibility, and professional growth. You are asked to critically assess your own PLN and consider how AI is changing the landscape of networked learning.

### Required Materials

- READ: [Building AI Literacy in Pre-Service Teacher Education in Canada - Estaiteyeh & Mindzak](./estaiteyeh-mindzak-ai-literacy.md)

### Requirements (merged from both sources)

**From Course Outline:**
- Reflect on your current PLN: which networks, tools, or communities you engage with (e.g., BlueSky, LinkedIn, EdTech communities, provincial teacher networks)
- Reflect on how these networks support your professional learning goals
- **Include at least one Canadian source** that discusses PLNs in the Canadian educational context (e.g., provincial or national teacher professional development)
- Critically assess the strengths and weaknesses of your PLN and the inclusion of AI
- Consider aspects such as diversity of perspectives, relevance, accessibility, and quality of resources

**From Instructor Posts Page:**
- Identify current PLN networks/tools; assess strengths/weaknesses
- Incorporate Canadian source discussing PLNs in education
- **Hint:** Research Chris Kennedy (West Vancouver Schools Superintendent) as a possible Canadian source

### Key Concepts

- **AI Literacy:** Understanding AI capabilities, limitations, and ethical implications in educational contexts
- **PLN Assessment:** Critically evaluating the diversity, relevance, and quality of your learning network
- **Canadian context:** How Canadian educational policy and practice intersect with AI and PLNs
- **Professional growth through PLNs:** How networked learning supports ongoing career development
