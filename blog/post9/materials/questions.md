## Questions to Address in Blog Post #9

### From Course Outline

1. Is personal data being collected without explicit educational purpose?
2. Are third-party analytics compliant with Canadian privacy standards?
3. Do students have meaningful control over their information?

### From Instructor Posts Page

4. Address PLN data harvesting implications.
5. Consider the ethical, educational, and social implications of data collection in PLNs.
6. Emphasize the Canadian context -- PIPEDA, provincial privacy laws, and the jurisdictional patchwork.

### Additional Considerations

7. How do the platforms you use in your PLN collect and use your data?
8. What are the privacy implications of using social media for professional learning?
9. How does PIPEDA apply to educational technology tools?
10. What role do provincial privacy laws (e.g., BC's FIPPA) play in protecting student data?
11. How should these privacy considerations be incorporated into your final group project?

### Suggested Reflection Points

- Connect the DAIS Generation AI report's findings about youth privacy risks to your own PLN experience
- Consider the PowerSchool breach case study -- what does it mean for educational data security?
- Reflect on the 10 PIPEDA principles and how they apply to the platforms you use
- Think about the tension between the benefits of open, networked learning and the privacy costs
- Consider how Privacy by Design principles could improve the platforms you use
