## Core Thesis

As AI tools become ubiquitous in schools and social media, young people face unprecedented privacy risks. Governments, technologists, educators, and parents must implement concrete safeguards immediately.

## Seven Key Privacy Risks Identified

1. **Unconsented Data Collection:** Young users unknowingly share personal information with AI platforms that retain and repurpose their data without clear understanding of consequences.
2. **Profiling and Targeted Advertising:** AI systems create persistent data profiles enabling automated targeting and behavioral influence.
3. **Cybersecurity Vulnerabilities:** Educational institutions and AI providers experience data breaches exposing student records, health information, and location data.
4. **Educational Surveillance:** Classroom adoption of genAI enables continuous tracking of learning patterns, behaviors, and personal interests.
5. **Bias and Misinformation:** AI trained on skewed datasets reinforces harmful stereotypes and generates misleading information.
6. **Harmful Content Creation:** Bad actors exploit image and video generation tools to create deepfake CSAM and cyberbullying content.
7. **Manipulative Design:** "Dark patterns" subtly encourage excessive data sharing and engagement, exploiting youth vulnerabilities.

## Policy Recommendations

### For Policymakers
- Establish youth-specific privacy protections with explicit consent requirements
- Ban data collection without affirmative parental/youth consent
- Create enforceable penalties for corporate privacy violations
- Develop clear AI-in-education guidelines

### For Educators
- Conduct Privacy Impact Assessments before adopting AI tools
- Integrate AI literacy into K-12 curricula
- Establish institutional AI usage guidelines

## Canadian Legal Landscape

**Federal Level:** PIPEDA governs private sector data handling; proposed Bill C-27 (died in 2025) sought comprehensive modernization with youth protections.
**Provincial Advances:** Ontario's Bill 194 (November 2024) first provincial law specifically governing AI in education with minor-specific protections.

## Notable Case Studies

- **PowerSchool breach (2024):** Toronto District School Board hack exposed medical records across two decades
- **Character.AI data breach:** Usernames, voice recordings, and chat histories compromised
- **AI Companion Risks:** October 2024 death of 14-year-old linked to intense emotional attachment with companion AI chatbot
