## Full Content

**Date:** October 27, 2025

MediaSmarts launched its 20th annual Media Literacy Week (October 27-31, 2025) with a new campaign titled "Wait... What?" designed to teach critical thinking about AI-generated online content. The initiative encourages Canadians to ask critical questions about content, verify information, identify biases, and consider before sharing.

### Key Statistics

- **Two-thirds** of Canadians were exposed to AI-generated misinformation multiple times in the past year
- **1 in 3** Canadians used generative AI tools in the past year, more than doubling since 2024
- **74 percent** of Canadians are concerned about misinformation created by AI tools
- Last year's Media Literacy Week generated **88.5 million online impressions** with **175 collaborating organizations**

### Campaign Details

Executive Director Kathryn Ann Hill noted: "Online content has changed dramatically since we began Media Literacy Week 20 years ago." The campaign responds to escalating concerns about deepfakes and AI-generated misinformation becoming increasingly prevalent online.

The week includes the fourth annual Digital Citizen Day on October 29, promoting respectful and responsible online behavior.

### Institutional Support

The initiative operates under Canadian Commission for UNESCO patronage and receives sponsorship from Bell, Google, Meta, TELUS Wise, TikTok Canada, and YouTube, with additional support from various provincial teachers' associations.
