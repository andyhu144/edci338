## Article Summary

Published March 9, 2026 by CBC News Investigates.

### Key Findings

- Members of the Canadian Armed Forces had accounts on WhiteDate, a white-only dating site that promoted white supremacist ideology
- WhiteDate, launched in 2017, explicitly marketed itself as a white-only dating platform, welcomed white supremacist and neo-Nazi members, and has been referred to as "Tinder for Nazis" by extremism experts
- In December, an anonymous hacker known as Martha Root shut down WhiteDate and published its data, exposing thousands of users worldwide on a site called OKStupid
- CBC News found more than 500 accounts based in Canada; roughly 200 have been matched to real people
- Among WhiteDate's Canadian users, CBC News discovered three members who identify themselves on social media as military personnel
- The Department of National Defence is investigating
