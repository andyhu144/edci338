## Questions to Address in Blog Post #8

### From Course Outline

1. What are the specific risks and opportunities AI introduces for digital citizenship within Canadian education?
2. What role does media literacy education play in empowering learners to critically assess AI-generated content, misinformation, and extremist narratives online?
3. How effective are current Canadian policies and educational initiatives at addressing AI-driven misinformation and online radicalization?

### From Instructor Posts Page

4. Describe an example of AI-amplified misinformation observed on Canadian social media.
5. Explain its potential impact on public opinion.
6. Link your reflection to one of the provided resources.
7. Propose and justify a media literacy curriculum segment that teaches learners/employees to critically assess AI-driven social media content.
8. Support your thoughts with Canadian policy or research evidence.

### Assignment Requirements

9. Include a specific Canadian example of AI-amplified misinformation.
10. Propose a media literacy curriculum segment with justification.
11. Reference at least one of the provided DAIS reports or MediaSmarts resource.
12. Connect your analysis to Canadian policy frameworks (e.g., PIPEDA, Bill C-27, provincial initiatives).

### Suggested Reflection Points

- The DAIS "Human or AI?" report finding that small labels are no different from leaving content unlabeled -- what does this mean for platform accountability?
- MediaSmarts statistics showing 2/3 of Canadians exposed to AI misinformation -- how does this affect democratic discourse?
- The Generation AI report's seven privacy risks for youth -- which are most relevant to your PLN?
- The CBC investigation into WhiteDate -- how does this demonstrate the intersection of digital identity, data breaches, and social consequences?
- Vass Bednar's argument about AI slop threatening Canadian culture -- how does this connect to your PLN curation?
