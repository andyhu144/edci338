## Overview

This is a self-assessment quiz from UBC's Digital Tattoo project with 12 questions designed to help users evaluate their digital identity awareness and online safety practices.

## Quiz Questions Summary

**Question 1: Digital Identity Perspective**
Three response options address different comfort levels with online presence, from unconcerned to privacy-conscious to completely offline. Feedback directs users to tutorials on computer security, mobile protection, and identity theft prevention.

**Question 2: Best Aspect of Being Online**
Options cover social media, financial transactions, and personal branding. Responses educate about data ownership, web trackers, mobile security, and cultivating professional online presence.

**Question 3: Primary Online Concern**
Addresses cyberbullying, identity theft, and computer viruses. Feedback provides resources on harassment, privacy settings, and malware protection.

**Question 4: Convenience vs. Privacy Trade-off**
Explores attitudes toward data collection and targeted advertising. Guidance covers cookie management and browser security settings.

**Question 5: Managing Online Identity Difficulty**
Assesses confidence in digital identity curation, with feedback emphasizing that "information put online is very difficult to remove" and directing to profile management resources.

**Question 6: Public WiFi Usage**
Tests understanding of security risks when using unsecured networks. Responses explain proper precautions for sensitive transactions.

**Question 7: Photo Uploading Practices**
Addresses metadata, geotagging, and location privacy risks in digital images.

**Question 8: Blogs and Wikis Perspective**
Evaluates familiarity with collaborative online tools and their educational applications.

**Question 9: Online Dating Attitudes**
Discusses meeting people through digital platforms with nuanced perspectives on safety and opportunity.

**Question 10: Text vs. Phone Communication**
Explores understanding that texting creates permanent digital records unlike phone conversations.

**Question 11: File-Sharing and Piracy**
Examines knowledge of copyright laws and legal digital content access alternatives.

**Question 12: Online Gaming Perception**
Addresses stereotypes and healthy digital balance considerations.

All feedback includes hyperlinked resources to relevant tutorial pages on privacy, security, and digital citizenship.
