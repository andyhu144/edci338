## Definitions for the Visitors and Residents Mapping Exercise

**Visitor:** Functional use of technology, often linked to formal need (such as use of software for specific coursework, or organising meetings through email contact); less visible/more passive online presence, more likely to favor face-to-face interactions (even as they use the internet to organize/schedule those interactions).

**Resident:** Significant online presence and usage; high level of collaborative activity online; contributions to the online environment in the form of uploading materials, photos, videos.

## Context is Important

We often behave differently when we are using the Internet in a Personal context versus an Institutional (work-related) context, and sometimes we use it the same. For example, Facebook may be where you hang out with your friends online and you are a Resident because you leave lots of social traces as you interact with everyone. When you use Google to search, you do it as a Visitor. A search engine is a tool that you use to search for things, and when you use it you don't want to leave traces behind. You use it at home (Personal), at work (Institutional), and everywhere in between. But no matter where you are, you always use a search engine as a Visitor.

## V&R Map Axes

The map uses two axes:
- **Horizontal axis:** Visitor (left) to Resident (right)
- **Vertical axis:** Personal (top) to Institutional/Professional (bottom)

Place your digital tools and platforms on this map according to how you use them.
