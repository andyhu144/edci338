## Book Overview

*Tell Everyone* by Alfred Hermida examines why people share information on social media and the implications of this sharing behavior for journalism, democracy, and society. Hermida, a professor of journalism at UBC, draws on research in social psychology, communication theory, and digital media studies.

## Key Themes

- Why humans are compelled to share information
- The psychology of social sharing behavior
- How social media has transformed journalism and news distribution
- The impact of citizen journalism and participatory media
- The role of trust in information sharing
- How algorithms shape what we see and share
- The democratization of information production and distribution
- Implications for professional journalists and media organizations

## Relevance to EDCI 338

Hermida's work connects directly to PLN development by examining the social dynamics that drive information sharing. Understanding why people share helps explain how PLNs form, grow, and function as tools for professional learning.
