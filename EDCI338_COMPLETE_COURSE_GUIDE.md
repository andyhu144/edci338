## 1. COURSE OVERVIEW

**Calendar Description:** EDCI 338 examines the application of social media in educational contexts and its relationship to personalized learning. The course utilizes blogs and other social media tools to develop networked literacy and learning networks that contribute to professional practice in legal, safe, consensual, and ethical ways.

**Format:** Online, primarily asynchronous. Optional weekly synchronous Zoom sessions (time TBD).

**Course Delivery:** NOT delivered via Brightspace. WordPress updates and Mattermost communication are the primary tools.

**Course Website:** https://edtechuvic.ca/edci338/category/jmiller/

### Learning Objectives
Upon completion, learners will be able to:
1. Examine the application of social media to support learning
2. Explain personalized learning and its relation to learning theory
3. Recognize that the Internet is not a neutral or equal space for everyone
4. Apply social media to develop a personal learning network (PLN) in support of personal and/or professional development
5. Identify privacy issues and practice online behaviours that are legal, safe, consensual, and ethical
6. Recognize data ownership and licensing (Creative Commons, Copyright, Fair Dealing, and Public Domain) as a core practice when creating, sharing, or using resources online
7. Practice digital, networked, and open literacies in support of learning about social media and personalized learning

### Blog Post #9: Privacy Compliance (Week 11 -- Mar 16)
**Topic:** PIPEDA, Privacy Regulations, and Data Practices in PLNs

**Context:** In Canada, privacy regulations like PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act) govern how organizations collect, use, and disclose personal information. Educational exceptions and patchwork provincial laws mean that data practices in PLNs often fall into unclear jurisdictional spaces.

**Questions to consider:**
- Is personal data being collected without explicit educational purpose?
- Are third-party analytics compliant with Canadian privacy standards?
- Do students have meaningful control over their information?

**Requirements:**
Consider that PLNs offer transformative potential for professional learning and collaboration, but these benefits come with embedded systems of data harvesting and third-party collection that have ethical, educational, and social implications. A critical, reflective, and policy-informed approach is essential especially in the Canadian context. Create a blog post reflective of the questions above and **incorporate these themes into your final project**.

## 11. KEY CONCEPTS & THEORIES

### Core Concepts
1. **Personal Learning Networks (PLN)** -- The central concept of the entire course. A PLN is a network of people, resources, and tools that support ongoing professional learning through social media and digital platforms.

2. **Digital Identity** -- The online persona created through social media activity. Includes both controlled (what you post) and uncontrolled (what others post about you) elements. Personal vs. professional digital identity management.

3. **Visitor & Resident (V&R) Framework** -- White & Le Cornu's framework distinguishing between:
   - **Visitors:** Transactional, task-based use of digital platforms (go in, complete task, leave)
   - **Residents:** Community-oriented, identity-building presence (leave a social trace)

4. **Networked Publics** -- Public spaces restructured by networked technologies; how people participate, share, and communicate in digital environments.

5. **Media Literacy** -- The ability to critically analyze, evaluate, and create media; understanding how media messages are constructed and their impact.

6. **Digital Citizenship** -- Responsible, ethical, and legal use of technology and digital platforms.

7. **Data Privacy & PIPEDA** -- Canadian privacy regulations (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act) and their implications for educational technology and PLNs.

8. **AI Literacy** -- Understanding how AI intersects with social media, misinformation, and digital citizenship in Canadian education.

9. **Inclusion & Diversity in PLNs** -- Ensuring learning networks represent diverse voices and perspectives; using social media for inclusive professional messaging.

10. **Open Licensing** -- Creative Commons, Copyright, Fair Dealing, and Public Domain as core practices for creating, sharing, and using resources online.

### Theoretical Foundations
- **Connectivism** -- Learning theory emphasizing networks and connections (underpins PLN concept)
- **Social Constructivism** -- Knowledge is constructed through social interaction
- **Networked Learning** -- Learning facilitated by connections between people and resources
- **Digital Literacies** -- Multiple competencies required for meaningful participation in digital environments
- **Personalized Learning** -- Tailoring learning experiences to individual needs, interests, and pace

### Canadian Context Themes (Weeks 9-11)
- Canadian educational policy on AI and digital citizenship
- PIPEDA and provincial privacy regulations (e.g., BC's FIPPA)
- Canadian teacher professional development through PLNs
- MediaSmarts and Canadian media literacy initiatives
- Data sovereignty and jurisdictional challenges in educational technology
